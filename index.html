Using retained profits instead of dividends

Most high street lenders only count salary and dividends actually drawn. A keeps profits in the company — which is tax-efficient but makes declared personal income look lower. A small number of lenders assess salary + share of net company profit, which can materially increase the accepted income figure.

Current rates — March 2026
Rate environment has shifted
Fixed rates were edging down through early 2026, then the Middle East conflict pushed swap rates up. NatWest, TSB and Virgin Money have hiked twice in two weeks. All lenders have withdrawn deals under 4%. BoE held at 3.75% on 19 March — next decision 30 April. A rate available today may not be available next week.
Why 80% LTV matters

Lenders price in bands — 60%, 75%, 80%, 85%, 90%. 80% is a hard boundary. Going above it moves to the 85% tier, typically 0.2–0.4% more per year. On a large mortgage that's £800–£1,600 extra annually for the same property.

Where rates might go in 2026

Before March's shock the consensus was 1–2 BoE cuts bringing the base rate to 3.25–3.5% and fixed deals nudging toward 3.8–4.2%. That outlook has been disrupted near-term.

Best case
3.8–4.2%

What to do
Lock in a rate as soon as an offer is accepted — most lenders hold for 3–6 months. Given current volatility a 5-year fix is the lower-risk choice. A 2-year fix bets on rates falling significantly by 2028.

Section 4
Ownership
The split starts at the deposit ratio and moves over time as contributions accumulate — always reflecting what each person has actually put in.
How it works
Ownership is based on total contributions — deposit paid at completion, plus every mortgage payment made from that point. Add up what each person has paid, divide by the combined total, and that's the split. The starting position reflects the deposits. Every month it updates to reflect reality.
Florin's share only goes up
Because both parties are contributing each month, Florin's share grows continuously as he pays in. It starts at his deposit proportion and increases over time. If he wants to accelerate that — lump sum, higher monthly — his share moves faster. There's no ceiling.
The evidence is just bank statements

Nobody needs to track anything actively. The standing orders do the work. At the point of sale — or any time either person wants to know the current split — you add up deposits plus all mortgage payments from each person's account and do the division. The Deed of Trust specifies that bank statements are the evidence. That's it. In practice you'd only ever calculate it when you actually need the number.
